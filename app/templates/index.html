Introduction

Biking in NYC has been on the rise especially since the establishment of Citibike in 2013. Unfortunately, with the rise in bike ridership, there is also a rise in cyclist related accidents. Particularly this year, there appears to be a rise in fatal cyclist accidents despite the increase in cyclist and pedestrian infrastructure throughout the city. I also have a personal investment in this as a regular bike commuter myself.

A deep dive into the data would provide insight into where cyclist infrastructure could either be constructed or improved upon depending on two main factors: the frequency of cyclist related accidents in different municipal locations as well as the most commonly used cyclist routes in the city. A closer look at where currently established bike lanes exist including the type should also provide useful insight.

The Data

Bike Census and Common Routes

To get a census of ridership, the CitiBike system data will be used as a proxy. Though not perfect, this should serve as a decent substitute for the overall bike commuter population in NYC. To get a sense of where these bikes are going, I'll be going under an assumption and will be using the Mapbox Directions API to find common routes.

Bike Lanes

I'll be getting bicycle lane data from NYC OpenData and differentiate the protected vs. unprotected lanes.

Accident Data

Bicycle related accidents will come from NYC OpenData and is defined as accidents that have at least 1 injured or killed cyclist.
